Web Site Critiques

Questions for Web Site Evaluation

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information?
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Pitchfork - http://pitchfork.com/

My first impression of the Pitchfork site is that it looks modern, clean, and very focused on music journalism. The homepage immediately highlights major reviews and news stories, which makes it feel like an online magazine.

In terms of how the site organizes the flow of information, it uses a straightforward approach with a top navigation bar linking to Reviews, News, Best New Music, and other sections. Most pages follow a consistent layout with large images at the top and text content below.

The site structure makes sense because everything is grouped by category, and the navigation stays in the same place on every page. It feels organized rather than confusing.

The site's graphics and layout enhance the overall experience because the large album covers and artist photos make the content visually appealing. The design is simple enough that the images stand out without being overwhelming.

Regarding load time, the homepage loads quickly, and the amount of images and text feels reasonable for a media site. Nothing on the page slows it down noticeably.

The text is highly readable, with black or dark gray fonts on a white background. The font size is comfortable, and the minimal color scheme keeps everything easy to see.

For navigation, the site uses a clear navigation bar at the top, and it works well. It stays consistent across pages, making it easy to move between sections.

In terms of visual consistency, Pitchfork uses a simple, repeated color scheme of white backgrounds, black text, and occasional accent colors for highlights. This gives the site a unified look and feel across all pages.

When it comes to responsive design, the site responds well when the window size gets smaller. The navigation collapses into a mobile-style menu, and the content stacks vertically, which keeps it usable on small screens.

What Pitchfork does especially well is its use of large, high-quality images paired with clean typography. It creates a professional, magazine-style experience that feels polished and easy to browse.
